About Staffies

Popularity

To know the breed is to love the breed. Staffies are a best friend, a soul mate. Previously, as staffies came out of unstable homes, a stigma as an aggressive dog was attached to them and they began to fill dog pounds and rescue centres. Thankfully, this has since been turned around and the breed is now a common sight in homes and in public on dog walks. They are fast becoming one of the more popular dog breeds in Ireland.

Charactertistics

Staffies are confident, smart and known for their love of people and trustworthy nature. They even carry the nickname "nanny dog" due to their reputation as a child's playmate and guardian. If you are looking for a devoted loyal and connected dog, introduce yourself to a Staffie and their owner and immediately you will witness their open hearts and how they centre their world on their people.

The Staffie breed is caring, loving, and will try to talk to you and join in with your conversation. They really identify closely with humans and try to communicate with their loved ones. This is called Staffy speak. They can make little nasal grunts and snore the night through. They are charming in their need to offer you what they have and are very trainable. Staffies are muscular. Like the British bull dog they are heavily muscled which, when you own one and know their kindness, you tend to forget. Others seeing their muscular cheeks tend to be nervous at first. You however soon translate that into the smile and laughter of the Staffy as their personality shines through.

Sociability

Their sociability with other dogs is broadly based, like all dogs, on being highly socialised with dogs from an early age. Not all staffies that come into our care have been well socialised from a young age but with time and effort, it can be managed and improved.
